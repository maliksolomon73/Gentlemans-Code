The Story

Prologue

John August never wanted to be king. He only wanted control.

Raised by a mother who believed dignity was wealth and silence was strength, John learned early that the loudest men rarely lasted. In a city ruled by impulsive violence and inherited power, he builds something different — an empire founded not on chaos, but on restraint.

He doesn't shout. He doesn't posture. And he never moves without purpose.

As rival crews clash for territory and old-world crime families begin circling his growing influence, John discovers a dangerous truth: order is more threatening than brutality. The disciplined man unsettles the reckless. The quiet one reshapes the room.

But power draws attention.

When betrayals surface from within, when blood is spilled without permission, and when the shadow of his father's past resurfaces to test his loyalty and legacy, John must decide whether he will inherit the old system — or dismantle it entirely.

In a world where violence is easy and fear is temporary, John August becomes something far more dangerous: a man who understands that real power is not how loudly you strike — but how precisely.

Gentleman's Code is a gripping crime saga about legacy, discipline, and the cost of building order in a world that profits from chaos.

Some men rule through fear.
Others rule because they don't need to.

The Author
